The gesture gate turns user input into a frost-shader seed, virtual scroll drives stage lifecycle from one value, texture uploads are pulled forward through an idle queue, and quality tiers lower pixel ratio, blur samples, and text resolution without changing the story. Connected to the recent SOG retry / fog / audio work, loading and quality drops can become visible reactions such as frost, fog, and grain shifts instead of hidden problems.

制作に使えそうな技術メモProduction notes

実装では、chunk や texture を直接待つのではなく、状態 texture として可視化するのが扱いやすいです。R=stale hitch residue、G=warmed/uploaded quality、B=queued retry/cooldown、A=validity とし、別 uniform で quality tier と upload pressure を渡します。three.js 側なら renderer.initTexture() や shader prewarm のタイミングを event 化し、PlayCanvas / SuperSplat 側なら Streamed SOG の LOD meta / chunk load / unload / cache miss を channel 化します。Babylon.js 9.17.0 の retry cooldown と safe-orbit limits は、camera が寄りすぎた時だけ B channel を増やすと視覚的に説明しやすいです。ZERO の lesson は、KTX2/DRACO/atlas 圧縮だけでなく、GPU upload の発生タイミングを作品設計に含めることです。

For production, visualize chunk and texture state as a state texture instead of waiting on raw events. Use R = stale hitch residue, G = warmed/uploaded quality, B = queued retry/cooldown, and A = validity, with uniforms for quality tier and upload pressure. In three.js, emit events around renderer.initTexture() and shader prewarming; in PlayCanvas / SuperSplat, channel Streamed SOG LOD metadata, chunk load, unload, and cache misses. Babylon.js 9.17.0 retry cooldown and safe-orbit limits can increase the B channel only when the camera pushes too close. ZERO's lesson is to design not just KTX2/DRACO/atlas compression, but the timing of GPU upload itself.
